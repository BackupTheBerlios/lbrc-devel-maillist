self.logger = logger.getLogger('LBRC.Listener.DBUSCall')

self.logger = logging.getLogger('LBRC.Listener.DBUSCall')

Hey,

I had a look at the python-xlib bindings and I like what I see. The
generation of fake keypresses and motion events seems to be much less
problematic, than the uinput approach. So I would like you to tell me
what you think about it. Ok here we go:

Uinput
======
Pro:
      * Works indepentently of X and also on the command line
      * could be started on system level

Con:
      * Mapping between KeyCode and generated Char is unknown on
        kernel-level (but the locale mapping is already done by the j2me
        part => every none english keyboard will produce "wrong" codes)
      * Needs self build uinput bridge (another running process)
      * Needs a build stage

X11
===
Pro:
      * get rid of the uinput bridge (was nice to get to know the uinput
        layer of the kernel, but is mighty ugly)
      * inject correctly localiced codes
      * drops dependency on build process

Cons:
      * see pros of Uinput
      * introduces new dependency on python-x11

My opinion leads to the X11 variant as it feels much less hasle. But
please voice your opinion!
